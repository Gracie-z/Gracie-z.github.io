I’m a third-year Statistics PhD student at Stanford University, advised by Sourav Chatterjee and Amir Dembo. Prior to Stanford, I got my B.S. in Honors Mathematics and Data Science from NYU Shanghai/NYU, where I was advised by Wei Wu and Chenlin Gu.

My research focuses broadly on probability theory, with particular emphasis on problems arising from statistical physics. My current work explores topics including Yang–Mills theory, random walks, and Liouville quantum gravity.

I have also become interested in AI4Math and AI more broadly — see my AI4Math page. If you share similar interests, feel free to reach out!
